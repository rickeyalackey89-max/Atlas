Thought for 21s
In Atlas, q_blowout and fragility are different layers of the same story:

q_blowout is an input-side game-state risk

fragility is an output-side probability gap

q_blowout in Atlas
q_blowout is the model’s estimate of the probability that the game margin becomes extreme enough to count as a blowout. Atlas computes it from the game spread and a threshold using a two-tailed Normal tail probability. In code, that is:

get a robust spread from the row

compute q = blowout_probability(spread_mean=spread, threshold=blowout_threshold, sd=spread_sd) 

 


So mathematically, q_blowout is:

a probability in [0, 1]

driven by spread, blowout threshold, and spread standard deviation

independent of whether the prop is OVER or UNDER

It is basically Atlas saying:

“How likely is this game to get far enough out of hand that minutes / game script distort the normal projection environment?”

fragility in Atlas
fragility is not computed from spread directly. It is computed from the difference between two probabilities:

p_adj: the main leg probability after blowout/minutes adjustment on the core leg path

p_close_adj: the close-game adjusted probability on the close-only channel

In the cleaner probability file, Atlas computes:

frag = max(0, (p_close_adj - p_adj) / p_close_adj)

frag_abs = max(0, p_close_adj - p_adj) 


So mathematically, fragility is:

relative gap between the close-game world and the final adjusted world

zero if blowout/minutes effects barely matter

larger when the final adjusted probability gets dragged down far below the close-game probability

Operationally, Atlas is saying:

“How much probability did I lose because this leg is sensitive to the non-close / blowout environment?”

The relationship between them
They are connected, but they are not the same variable.

q_blowout
Measures:

how likely the risky game environment is

fragility
Measures:

how much that risky environment actually changes the leg probability

That distinction matters.

You can have:

High q_blowout, lower fragility
If a blowout is fairly likely, but the prop/stat/player is not very minutes-sensitive, then the game-state risk is there, but the leg does not move much.

Lower q_blowout, higher fragility
If the game-state risk is only modest, but the prop is very sensitive to minutes/game flow, then even a modest blowout probability can create a meaningful probability gap.

So in Atlas terms:

q_blowout is the risk of the event

fragility is the damage that event does to the leg probability

Where each one enters the pipeline
Atlas first builds the raw and role-context probabilities from simulation:

p_raw

p_role 


Then it applies blowout/minutes sensitivity to p_role:

p_adj = adjust_probability_for_blowout(p_raw=p_role, blowout_risk=q, sens=minutes_s) 

 


It also builds a close-only adjusted path with reduced blowout sensitivity:

minutes_s_close = minutes_s * close_sens_mult

p_close_adj = adjust_probability_for_blowout(..., sens=minutes_s_close) 


Then fragility is computed from the gap between those two adjusted paths. 


So the dependency is:

spread → q_blowout → p_adj and p_close_adj → fragility

That means q_blowout is upstream; fragility is downstream.

The cleanest plain-English distinction
If I had to say it in one sentence:

q_blowout = “How likely is the blowout script?”

fragility = “How much does that script hurt this specific leg?”

Why this matters for your current idea
This is exactly why your “reduced haircut for qualifying UNDERS” idea is well-formed.

Because if you reduce the blowout haircut for certain UNDERS, you are changing the q_blowout → p_adj effect.

You are not changing what fragility means.
Fragility will still be the observed downstream gap — it will just be smaller if the haircut is less severe.

So if you want the most natural seam for the math change, it is:

use fragility and q_blowout as gates

reduce the blowout haircut applied to p_adj

let fragility remain a measured consequence, not the thing being directly “boosted”

That is more faithful to the current Atlas math.
